# Continuity Requires Operational Structure

## Concept

Continuity is not created by memory alone. It requires a structure that keeps
intent, state, constraints, review boundaries, and next actions legible over
time.

## Operational Pattern

AI-assisted workflows can produce useful artifacts while still losing the shape
of the work around them. Without structure, the output exists, but the reason it
exists, its current status, and its approval boundary become harder to inspect.

## Design Principle

A continuity system should make work resumable without turning previous context
into automatic authority. The structure should help humans review, decide, and
correct direction.

## Public Model

Useful continuity asks four questions:

- What is the current objective?
- What constraints still apply?
- What has already been reviewed?
- What decision remains human-controlled?

## Boundary

The goal is not to remove supervision. The goal is to make supervision easier
to perform consistently.
