# Public Repositories Are Distillation Surfaces

## Concept

A public knowledge repository should not mirror a private operating system. It
should distill lessons that remain useful after private mechanisms are removed.

## Why It Matters

Literal publication can expose architecture, strategy, and operational details
that were never meant to become public.

## Public Model

Internal learning can inspire public knowledge only after classification,
sanitization, abstraction, routing, and human review.

## Boundary

Public knowledge should preserve the lesson, not the private machinery that
produced it.
